Here are some advanced terminology and topics that will help in the understanding of the vast crypto world:

Decentralized Finance

Decentralized finance (DeFi) refers to a system where financial products and services are built on blockchain networks, making them accessible to anyone with
an internet connection and eliminating the need for traditional intermediaries like banks. Key concepts include decentralized applications (DApps), smart contracts,
and protocols that enable activities such as lending, borrowing, trading, and earning interestâ€”all in a transparent and often permissionless manner.

Staking and Yield Farming

Staking: Staking involves locking up cryptocurrency to support a blockchain network's operations and earn rewards. It helps secure the network and validate transactions,
typically rewarding participants with additional tokens.

Yield Farming: Yield farming is a way to earn rewards with cryptocurrency holdings by providing liquidity to decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols. Users can earn interest,
fees, or governance tokens by lending or staking their assets on these platforms. It often involves moving assets between different protocols to maximize returns.

ICO/IEO Investments

ICO (Initial Coin Offering): An ICO is a fundraising method where new cryptocurrency projects sell their tokens to early investors in exchange for funding.
Investors hope the tokens will appreciate in value once the project launches.

IEO (Initial Exchange Offering): An IEO is similar to an ICO, but the token sale is conducted on a cryptocurrency exchange platform. The exchange typically vets
the project, manages the token sale, and lists the tokens for trading post-sale, providing a level of security and convenience for investors.
